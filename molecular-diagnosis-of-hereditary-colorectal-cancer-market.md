<p><p>Molecular diagnosis of hereditary colorectal cancer encompasses various techniques that identify genetic mutations associated with the disease. PCR (Polymerase Chain Reaction) amplifies specific DNA segments for analysis. FISH (Fluorescence In Situ Hybridization) detects chromosomal abnormalities using fluorescent probes. DNA sequencing provides detailed information about nucleotide sequences, revealing mutations. Gene chips (microarrays) allow simultaneous examination of multiple genes, facilitating the detection of hereditary syndromes. Together, these methods enhance early diagnosis and targeted treatment options for patients at risk.</p></p>
