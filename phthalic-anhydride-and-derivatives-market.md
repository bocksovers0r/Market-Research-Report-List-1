<p><p>Phthalic anhydride and its derivatives are primarily produced through two significant processes: O-xylene catalytic oxidation and naphthalene catalytic oxidation. The O-xylene catalytic oxidation method involves the oxidation of O-xylene in the presence of a catalyst, yielding higher purity and efficiency. In contrast, naphthalene catalytic oxidation utilizes naphthalene as a feedstock, generally offering a lower production cost. Both methods cater to the demand in industries such as plastics, paints, and coatings, highlighting their importance in the chemical market.</p></p>
