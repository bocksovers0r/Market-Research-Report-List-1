<p><p>Logistics management software is classified into two primary market types: cloud-based and on-premise solutions. Cloud-based logistics software is hosted on remote servers, allowing users to access it via the internet, promoting flexibility, scalability, and automatic updates. This model typically offers lower upfront costs and easier integration with other cloud services. In contrast, on-premise logistics software is installed locally on company servers, providing greater control over data and security but requiring significant upfront investment and ongoing maintenance.</p></p>
