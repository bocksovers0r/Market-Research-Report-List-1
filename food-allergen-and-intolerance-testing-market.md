<p><p>The food allergen and intolerance testing market includes three primary types: skin-based tests, blood tests, and alternative allergy tests. Skin-based tests involve the application of allergens to the skin, observing reactions for immediate hypersensitivity. Blood tests measure specific antibodies related to food allergens, providing a more controlled environment for diagnosis. Alternative allergy tests encompass various methodologies, such as hair analysis and enzyme-linked immunosorbent assays, offering diverse options for individuals seeking to identify food sensitivities or intolerances.</p></p>
