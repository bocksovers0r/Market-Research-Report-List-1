<p><p>Imaging technology for precision agriculture includes multispectral and hyperspectral technologies, each serving distinct purposes. Multispectral technology captures data across a few specific wavelengths, enabling farmers to analyze crop health, soil conditions, and water usage efficiently. Conversely, hyperspectral technology collects data across numerous wavelengths, offering detailed spectral information for advanced analysis of plant health, pest infestations, and nutrient levels. Both technologies enhance decision-making, optimize resource use, and ultimately improve crop yields while promoting sustainable farming practices.</p></p>
