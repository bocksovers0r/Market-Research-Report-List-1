<p><ul><li>Pneumatic Anti-Lock Braking System</li><li>Hydraulic Anti-Lock Braking System</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The automotive anti-lock braking system (ABS) market comprises two main types: pneumatic and hydraulic ABS. Pneumatic ABS uses compressed air to control braking pressure, typically found in heavy-duty vehicles. Hydraulic ABS, more common in passenger cars, utilizes hydraulic fluid and electronic sensors to modulate brake pressure, preventing wheel lock during braking. Both systems enhance vehicle safety and performance by allowing drivers to maintain steering control during emergency stops, thus reducing accident risks and improving overall driving dynamics.</p></p>
